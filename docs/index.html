About

The 26th European Agent Systems Summer School (EASSS 2026) will take place at Malmö University, Sweden, on 21–23 September 2026, under the auspices of EURAMAS, the European Association for Multi-Agent Systems. EASSS 2026 is hosted by the Department of Computer Science and Media Technology (DVMT) and the Sustainable Digitalisation Research Centre (SDRC) at Malmö University.

Since it began in 1999, EASSS has grown into one of the key meeting points in Europe for people working on autonomous agents and multi-agent systems, bringing together experienced researchers and early-career scientists in a focused, collaborative setting. The program is designed for advanced Master’s students, PhD candidates, and early-career researchers, offering tutorials led by experts that balance core foundations with the latest developments shaping the field.

Topics span both theory and real-world applications of distributed intelligent systems, from the Internet of Things and autonomous robotics to smart environments and large-scale AI systems, reflecting where the field is heading and where it is already making impact.

Call For Tutorials

The Organizing Committee of the 26th European Agent Systems Summer School (EASSS 2026) invites tutorial proposals for the event to be held at Malmö University, Sweden, on 21–23 September 2026. We are looking for engaging, well-structured tutorials that introduce key ideas in Autonomous Agents and Multi-Agent Systems while also highlighting emerging directions shaping the field today. Topics aligned with communities such as AAMAS and the JAAMAS journal are especially relevant.

Tutorials should provide a clear and balanced perspective on a topic, combining conceptual understanding with practical insight and real-world relevance. Each tutorial will run for approximately 3 hours (typically split into two 90-minute sessions) and is usually delivered by one or two instructors. We welcome contributions from both experienced researchers and early-career academics, aiming to create an interactive and high-quality learning experience for participants.

A contribution towards travel and accommodation may be provided for accepted tutorials.

Submission Instructions

Tutorial proposals must be submitted as a single PDF document (maximum 2 pages). Proposals should clearly communicate both the academic value and the learning experience of the tutorial. The expected structure follows established EASSS guidelines.

The proposal should include:

  1. Title of the tutorial
  2. Tutors (affiliation, contact details, personal webpages)
  3. Abstract (100–300 words, suitable for website publication)
  4. Structured outline of topics
  5. Type (theoretical, applied, or mixed)
  6. Target audience and prerequisites
  7. Teaching materials
  8. Duration (standard: 3 hours; justify deviations)
  9. Equipment requirements
  10. Short bios (~100 words per tutor)
  11. Relevant teaching experience

Tutorial Objectives

  • Introduce key areas of AAMAS research
  • Provide depth in established methodologies and techniques
  • Survey and critically assess mature or emerging topics
  • Motivate and position new research directions
  • Support hands-on understanding through practical or coding sessions
  • Bridge research with real-world and industry applications

Tutorial Format

  • Duration: 3 hours (two sessions of 90 minutes)
  • Format: lecture, interactive, or hands-on
  • Tutors: typically 1–2 presenters (larger teams must justify coherence)

Tutorials should be designed to engage a diverse audience, from advanced students to researchers entering a new area.

Scope and Topics

We welcome proposals across the full spectrum of Autonomous Agents and Multi-Agent Systems, including (but not limited to):

  • Foundations of multi-agent systems
  • Reinforcement learning and decision-making
  • Coordination, organizations, norms, and ethics
  • Agent-based modelling and simulation
  • Human-agent interaction and human-in-the-loop systems
  • Trustworthy and explainable AI
  • Agentic AI
  • Multi-agent learning and collective intelligence
  • LLM-based agents and hybrid architectures
  • Applications in robotics, healthcare, sustainability, and industry

We particularly encourage tutorials that connect established MAS principles with emerging paradigms, emphasize real-world impact, and include interactive or hands-on components.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Relevance and timeliness of the topic
  • Clarity and pedagogical quality
  • Expected interest for the EASSS audience
  • Expertise and experience of the tutors

Tutorials and Short Bios

Agent-Based Modeling, Social Simulations, Multi-Agent Systems, Emergence, NetLogo, Applications, and Policymaking Support

Tutors: Sz-Ting (Christine) Tzeng, Postdoctoral Researcher; Bertilla Fabris, PhD Student.

Affiliation: Umeå University, Sweden.

Short bio: Sz-Ting (Christine) Tzeng is a postdoctoral researcher at Umeå University. Bertilla Fabris is a PhD student at Umeå University. Their tutorial introduces agent-based modelling and social simulation, with attention to multi-agent systems, emergence, NetLogo-based modelling, applications, and policymaking support.

Multiagent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) for Traffic Control

Tutor: Ana Lúcia Cetertich Bazzan.

Affiliation: University of São Paulo, Polytechnic School, Brazil.

Short bio: Ana Lúcia Cetertich Bazzan’s PhD thesis focused on reinforcement learning and game-theoretic approaches to traffic signal control. From 1999 to 2024, she was a professor at UFRGS and led the MAS group there. After retiring in 2024, she continued at UFRGS as visiting professor and, since June 2026, she has also been a visiting professor at the Department of Transportation at the University of São Paulo. She is a well-known expert in MARL for traffic signal control, co-author of a book and surveys on the topic, and has served the AAMAS community, including as general co-chair of AAMAS 2014.

Formal Methods for Safe Reinforcement Learning

Tutors: Dr. Francesco Belardinelli, Senior Lecturer; Mr. Omar Adalat, PhD Student.

Affiliation: Imperial College London, United Kingdom.

Short bio: Francesco Belardinelli is a senior lecturer in the Department of Computing at Imperial College London, where he leads research on formal methods for AI. His work focuses on the specification and verification of complex AI systems, including safe reinforcement learning. He has organized several editions of the Summer School on Safe and Trusted AI and teaches modules related to symbolic AI, strategic reasoning, and formal methods for safe AI. Omar Adalat is a PhD student in the Department of Computing at Imperial College London. His research focuses on safe multi-agent learning with probabilistic and quantitative temporal logic constraints and learning from formal specifications. The tutorial has previously been delivered at AAAI 2025, ECAI 2025, and AAMAS 2026.

Multiagentic AI: Engineering Safe and Flexible Systems

Tutors: Amit K. Chopra, Senior Lecturer; Munindar P. Singh, SAS Institute Distinguished Professor.

Affiliations: Lancaster University, United Kingdom; North Carolina State University, USA.

Short bio: Amit K. Chopra is a senior lecturer at Lancaster University. His expertise lies in engineering abstractions for multiagent systems, including protocols, norms, and platforms, and his work has appeared in major AI venues including AAMAS, IJCAI, and AAAI. Munindar P. Singh is the SAS Institute Distinguished Professor of Computer Science at North Carolina State University. His research has shaped models of interaction, including formalizations of intentions, social commitments, and communication, and he has received major recognitions from AAAI, AAAS, ACM, IEEE, ACM SIGAI, and IFAAMAS.

Local Information

The Venue

EASSS 2026 will be held at Malmö University, located in southern Sweden. Malmö is Sweden’s third biggest city and can be easily reached by train, bus, car, and plane.

The summer school will take place in Malmö University’s Niagara building. Address: Nordenskiöldsgatan 1, 211 19 Malmö.

The venue can be accessed by the following means of transportation:

  • By plane:
    • Copenhagen Airport (Kastrup, EKCH) is a convenient choice with many direct routes. From the airport terminal, take the Öresund train to Malmö Central Station; the journey takes around 25 minutes.
    • Malmö Airport (Sturup, ESMS) is also available; take the airport bus to Malmö Central.
    • Copenhagen Kastrup is often the more convenient choice for travelling to Malmö.
  • By train:
    • From Malmö Central Station, Niagara is approximately a 5-minute walk.
    • Malmö can also be reached by night train from Berlin/Hamburg via Copenhagen.
  • By taxi: Uber and Bolt are widely used ride-sharing applications in Malmö.
  • By car: Parking garages are available nearby.
